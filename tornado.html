Tornado

What Is It?

A tornado is a long, narrow, spinning column of air that connects the thunderstorm’s clouds in the sky, to the ground. Most tornadoes only last for a couple of minutes, but some can last for hours and travel long distances!

How Does It Happen?

Tornadoes are born from supercell thunderstorms.

What is a supercell thunderstorm?
These types of thunderstorms contain a spinning column of air called a vortex. Vortexes cause warm air and moisture to rise, while the cool, dry air falls towards the ground. Once the funnel cloud touches the Earth’s surface, it becomes a tornado !

How Can I Prepare?

  • Stay up to date with weather surveillance systems, especially those that monitor tornadoes
  • Have a designated room, preferably with no windows, where you can get to quickly
